# Geoffrey Hinton

Geoffrey Hinton is a British-Canadian cognitive psychologist and computer scientist widely regarded as the "Godfather of Deep Learning." He is a professor at the University of Toronto and spent years at Google Brain. His decades of work on neural networks, backpropagation, and deep learning laid the foundation for the modern AI era. He won the Turing Award in 2018 (jointly with Yann LeCun and Yoshua Bengio).

## Key Facts
- Professor, University of Toronto; former VP & Engineering Fellow at Google Brain (2013–2023)
- Turing Award 2018 (with LeCun and Bengio) for foundational contributions to deep learning
- Supervised Alex Krizhevsky and Ilya Sutskever (AlexNet authors)
- Co-authored Dropout paper (2014) and Layer Normalization (2016)
- Left Google in 2023, citing concerns about AI safety risks
- Known for Boltzmann machines, capsule networks, backpropagation popularization
